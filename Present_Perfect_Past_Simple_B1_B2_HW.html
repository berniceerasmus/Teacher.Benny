Reading

A more complex professional text with analytical questions

B2

Before the current finance director joined the organisation, the company had been relying on outdated forecasting methods for several years. The previous team had attempted to modernise the process twice, but both initiatives had stalled due to insufficient budget and a lack of senior buy-in.

Since taking on the role eighteen months ago, the director has overhauled the entire forecasting framework. She has introduced automated reporting tools, trained the finance team on new software, and established a quarterly review process that did not exist before. As a result, forecast accuracy has improved by nearly 30%, and the finance team has been able to identify cash flow risks much earlier than in the past.

The director presented these results to the board last month. While the board responded positively, some members have raised questions about long-term sustainability, particularly given that two key team members have already left the company since the changes began, citing workload concerns.

The director is currently developing a retention strategy, though no formal plan has been approved yet.

1. What had the company been doing before the new director arrived, and why had earlier attempts to change this failed?

2. List three specific actions the director has taken since joining.

3. What concern have some board members raised, and what evidence supports it?

4. Identify two uses of the past perfect in the first paragraph. Why does the writer choose this tense rather than past simple?

5. What is still unresolved at the time of writing? How does the tense used make this clear?
