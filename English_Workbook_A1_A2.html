Reading — A Typical Day for Ayse

Ayse lives in Ankara with her family. She is a university student and she has a very busy daily routine.

She wakes up at 6:30 every morning. First, she has a shower and then she eats breakfast. She usually has bread, cheese, and a cup of tea. She never drinks coffee in the morning because it makes her feel nervous.

After breakfast, Ayse walks to the bus stop. She takes the bus to university and her first class starts at 9:00. She studies English and Business.

In the evenings, Ayse sometimes cooks dinner with her mother. After dinner, she reads or watches a short video online to practise her English. She goes to bed at about 11:00 every night.

Reading — Shopping at the Grand Bazaar

Last Saturday, Tom visited the Grand Bazaar in Istanbul for the first time. It is one of the largest and oldest covered markets in the world, with more than 4,000 shops inside.

Tom wanted to buy some souvenirs for his family. He found a small shop selling scarves, lamps, and ceramics. 'How much is this scarf?' he asked. 'It is 150 lira,' said the shopkeeper. Tom thought it was a little expensive. 'Is there a discount?' he asked. The shopkeeper smiled. 'For you, 120 lira - and I will give you a receipt.'

Tom also bought a small ceramic plate for his mother. There were many beautiful designs. He paid with cash and got some change back. He left the bazaar with four bags and a very big smile!

Grammar Practice
How much = uncountable nouns or price (How much milk? / How much is it?)
How many = countable nouns (How many apples?)
There is + singular | There are + plural
Negative: There isn't any ... | There aren't any ...

Reading — Getting Lost in Edinburgh

James is a tourist from Australia visiting Edinburgh, Scotland, for the first time. He is standing outside his hotel and he needs to find the castle.

He stops a local woman and asks for help. 'Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to Edinburgh Castle, please?' he asks.

'Of course!' she replies. 'Go straight along this road for about five minutes. At the traffic lights, turn left. Walk past the coffee shop - the castle is on the right, opposite the big park. You can't miss it - it is on top of a hill!'

'Is it far from here?' James asks. 'No, it is only about ten minutes on foot,' she says. James thanks her and follows the directions. When he arrives, he is amazed by how beautiful the castle is.

Reading — A Weekend in Antalya

Last weekend, Zeynep and her husband travelled to Antalya for a short holiday. They left Istanbul on Friday evening by plane and arrived at the hotel at around 9 o'clock at night.

On Saturday, they woke up early and went to the old city. They walked through the narrow streets, visited a beautiful mosque, and took lots of photos. In the afternoon, they went to the beach. The water was warm and clear. Zeynep swam for a long time but her husband preferred to sit in the sun and read his book.

In the evening, they found a small restaurant near the harbour. They ate fresh fish and salad and drank local lemonade. It was delicious! They got back to the hotel late and went straight to bed.

On Sunday, they visited the ancient ruins at Perge before flying home. Zeynep said it was one of the best weekends of her life.

Reading — At the Doctor's: A Difficult Day

David is 35 years old and works as a chef. This week, he has not been feeling well at all. He has had a terrible cough for five days and he has also had a high fever since Monday. He hasn't slept well and he hasn't been able to go to work.

This morning, David went to see his doctor. The doctor asked him several questions. 'Have you taken any medicine?' she asked. 'Yes, I have taken some cold medicine, but it hasn't helped much,' David replied. 'Have you ever had asthma or any breathing problems?' the doctor asked. 'No, never,' said David.

After the examination, the doctor told David that he had a chest infection. She gave him a prescription for antibiotics and told him to rest at home for at least three days. 'Drink plenty of water and avoid cold air,' she advised. David thanked her and went straight to the pharmacy to collect his medicine.

Reading — The Perfect Interview

Getting a job interview is exciting, but it can also be very stressful. Here are some tips that can help you to perform well and make a great impression on your employer.

Before the interview, you should research the company carefully. You must know what they do and why you want to work for them. You should also prepare answers to common questions such as 'Tell me about yourself' and 'What are your strengths?'

On the day of the interview, you must arrive on time - or better, a few minutes early. You should dress professionally and switch off your mobile phone. You don't have to wear a suit at every company, but you should always look neat and clean.

During the interview, you should speak clearly and confidently. You can ask the interviewer questions too - this shows that you are interested. After the interview, you could send a short thank-you email. This is not required, but it can make a very positive impression.
