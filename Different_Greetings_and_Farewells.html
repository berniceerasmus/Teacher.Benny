British and American English Tabs

British everyday

A: Hiya, you alright?
B: Yeah, not bad, thanks. You?
A: All good. See you in a bit.

British professional

A: Lovely to meet you, Ms. Brown.
B: Likewise. Thank you for coming in.
A: I’ll leave you to it. Kind regards.
B: Thank you. Have a pleasant afternoon.

American everyday

B: Not much. How about you?
A: Same here. Have a good one.
B: Thanks, see you around.

Situation-Based Mini Dialogues

General: New neighbour

A: Hi, I’m Deniz. I just moved in next door.
B: Nice to meet you! I’m Emma.
A: Nice to meet you too. See you around.
B: Definitely. Welcome to the building!

General: Friend at café

A: Hey! Long time no see.
B: I know! How have you been?
A: Pretty good. I should go, but let’s catch up soon.
B: Absolutely. Take care!

Business: Reception desk

A: Good morning. I have an appointment with Ms. Green.
B: Good morning. May I have your name, please?
A: Of course. Daniel Morris.
B: Thank you. Please take a seat.

Business: Networking

A: I don’t think we’ve met. I’m Lara from procurement.
B: Nice to meet you. I’m Omar from logistics.
A: How are you finding the event so far?
B: Very useful, especially the opening talk.

Business: Formal farewell

A: I don’t want to take up too much of your time.
B: Not at all. It was great speaking with you.
A: Likewise. Let’s stay in touch.
B: Absolutely. Have a safe journey back.

Online class / meeting

A: Hi everyone, can you hear me clearly?
B: Yes, we can.
A: Great. Welcome back. Let’s begin.
B: Sounds good.

Practice Tasks

Task 1 — Choose the better tone

  1. Your manager enters: “Yo!” / “Good morning.”
  2. You meet a new client: “Pleased to meet you.” / “What’s up?”
  3. You leave a friend: “Catch you later.” / “I look forward to our next meeting.”
  4. You end a formal call: “Thanks everyone. Have a good afternoon.” / “Anyway, bye.”

Task 2 — Complete the dialogue

  1. A: Good morning. My name is Ana. B: ________ to meet you.
  2. A: I need to leave now. B: It was lovely ________ with you.
  3. A: I don’t think we’ve ________ before. B: I’m James.
  4. A: Let’s ________ in touch. B: Definitely.

Task 3 — Make it more formal

  1. Hey, I’m Tom.
  2. I’ve got to run.
  3. Good chatting.
  4. Drop me a message.
  5. See ya.

Task 4 — Make it more informal

  1. It is a pleasure to meet you.
  2. I look forward to speaking again soon.
  3. Thank you for your time.
  4. Please do keep in touch.
  5. Have a pleasant afternoon.

Task 5 — Match the setting

  1. Reception desk
  2. Close friend
  3. Networking event
  4. Online lesson
  5. Business email ending

Students choose suitable greetings and farewells for each setting.

Task 6 — Pronunciation focus

  • Nice to meet you → /nais tuh MEE-chuh/
  • How’s it going? → /how-zit GO-ing/
  • See you later → /SEE-yuh LAY-tuh/
  • Good to see you → /GOOD-tuh SEE-yuh/

Role-Play Cards

A1 General

You meet a new classmate. Say hello, your name, ask “How are you?”, and say goodbye.

A2 General

You see a neighbour in the lift. Greet them, ask about their day, and end politely.

B1 General

You meet a friend you have not seen for two months. Greet, ask for news, and arrange to talk again.

B2 General

You meet your friend’s colleague at a dinner. Open warmly, ask follow-up questions, and exit naturally.

C1 General

You meet someone important at a cultural event. Open elegantly, make a connection, and close gracefully.

A1 Business

You greet a visitor at reception. Ask their name and say goodbye politely.

A2 Business

You welcome a guest to your office. Ask about their journey and offer help.

B1 Business

You meet a colleague from another department. Introduce yourself and suggest staying in touch.

B2 Business

You meet a conference speaker. Compliment their talk, ask a short question, and end politely.

C1 Business

You meet a senior executive. Open diplomatically, respect their time, and suggest continuing later.

Answer Key and Teacher Support

Suggested answers

Task 1: 1 Good morning. 2 Pleased to meet you. 3 Catch you later. 4 Thanks everyone. Have a good afternoon.

Task 2: 1 Nice / Pleased 2 speaking / talking 3 met 4 stay / keep

Task 3 examples: 1 Good morning, my name is Tom. 2 I’m afraid I need to leave now. 3 It was lovely speaking with you. 4 Please contact me when convenient. 5 Goodbye / Have a pleasant afternoon.

Task 4 examples: 1 Great to meet you. 2 Let’s talk again soon. 3 Thanks for your time. 4 Stay in touch. 5 Have a good one.

Extension ideas

Run a “tone ladder”: students rewrite one greeting from very informal to very formal. Then run a “speed networking” activity where students must greet, ask one follow-up question, and close the conversation in under one minute.
