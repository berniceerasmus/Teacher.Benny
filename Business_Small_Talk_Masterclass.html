How to use this resource

Small talk is not empty conversation. In business, it builds comfort, trust and professional relationships before, during and after the main work topic.

Students can open their level first, then move through situations. A1 and A2 include Turkish meanings written with the English alphabet only.

Teacher preview note

This is a review draft. It includes the full structure, representative vocabulary depth, dialogue models, role-play banks, and classroom tasks. After approval, the same design can be expanded even further or adjusted by industry, lesson length, or exam level.

Formal, Natural and International Tone Upgrades

Formal Business English

It is a pleasure to meet you.
Best for clients, senior managers, interviews and first meetings.

Natural Professional English

Great to finally meet you.
Best for colleagues, networking and warm workplace conversations.

International Workplace English

Nice to meet you. I work with the project team.
Clear, direct and safe for mixed-level international teams.

Industry-Specific Small Talk and Role Plays

General BusinessSales ManagementHRFinanceTech / SoftwareHospitalityHealthcareLogisticsR and DChemical EngineeringMining Engineering

Sales Management

Situation: You meet a regional sales manager before a product demo.
Dialogue goal: Ask about their market, team and current priorities without pushing the sale too early.

HR

Situation: You meet an HR manager at a recruitment fair.
Dialogue goal: Discuss onboarding, company culture and graduate opportunities.

Finance

Situation: You sit next to a finance analyst before a quarterly review.
Dialogue goal: Talk about workload, reporting cycles and busy periods.

Software Engineering

Situation: You meet a developer at a tech meetup.
Dialogue goal: Ask about their stack, product area and recent projects.

Hospitality

Situation: You greet a hotel operations manager before a partnership meeting.
Dialogue goal: Talk about guest experience, seasonality and service standards.

Healthcare

Situation: You meet a hospital administrator at a conference coffee break.
Dialogue goal: Discuss patient experience, systems and staff workload sensitively.

Logistics

Situation: You meet a logistics coordinator during a site visit.
Dialogue goal: Ask about routes, delays and warehouse operations.

Chemical R and D

Situation: You meet an R and D chemist before a lab tour.
Dialogue goal: Discuss innovation, safety and testing without asking confidential details.

Mining Engineering

Situation: You meet a mining engineer at an industry panel.
Dialogue goal: Discuss safety, technology and operations at a general level.

Model Dialogue — Tech / Software

A: Hi, I think we are in the same product session. I am Mert.

B: Nice to meet you, Mert. I am Laura. I work as a backend engineer.

A: Great. What kind of systems do you usually work on?

B: Mostly payment services and API integrations.

A: That must keep you busy, especially with security requirements.

B: Definitely. What about you?

A: I work with client implementation teams, so APIs come up quite often.

Classroom Tasks with Progressive Difficulty

Task 1 — Matching: phrase to function

  1. Nice to meet you. → a) End a conversation / b) First meeting / c) Ask about work
  2. What brings you here today? → a) Ask reason for attending / b) Offer coffee / c) Apologise
  3. I’ll let you get back to it. → a) Start conversation / b) End politely / c) Ask about travel
  4. How are you finding the conference? → a) Ask opinion / b) Make complaint / c) Introduce colleague
  5. Let me introduce you to our project manager. → a) Connect people / b) Disagree / c) Ask for a report
  6. It is great to put a face to the name. → a) Meet after emails/calls / b) Ask about salary / c) Talk about food
  7. Without getting into anything confidential... → a) Set boundary / b) Make joke / c) Say goodbye
  8. There may be scope for collaboration. → a) Suggest future cooperation / b) Ask for directions / c) Order lunch

Task 2 — Gap-fill: choose the best phrase

  1. I do not think we have been ________. I am Derya from finance.
  2. How are you ________ the conference so far?
  3. It is great to finally put a ________ to the name.
  4. I should let you get back ________ it.
  5. Would you like ________ coffee before we start?
  6. I have been meaning to ask you ________ the new system.
  7. That must keep you ________.
  8. Perhaps we can pick this ________ after the session.
  9. I would be curious to hear your ________.
  10. That is a delicate balance to ________.

Task 3 — Choose the best response

  1. A: How was your journey?
    a) I work in finance. b) Very smooth, thank you. c) I disagree.
  2. A: What brings you here today?
    a) I am attending the HR panel. b) It is on the table. c) Yes, I can hear you.
  3. A: How are you settling in?
    a) Pretty well, everyone has been helpful. b) Two coffees please. c) The deadline is Friday.
  4. A: What do you do in your role?
    a) I manage supplier relationships. b) I am fine thanks. c) It is raining.
  5. A: It was great speaking with you.
    a) I’ll let you get back to it. b) I am not responsible. c) Please attach it.
  6. A: Can I get you anything before we begin?
    a) A glass of water would be great, thank you. b) I graduated in 2021. c) The project failed.
  7. A: How did you get into logistics?
    a) I started in warehouse operations and moved into planning. b) I am sitting here. c) No, I did not.
  8. A: Which session did you find most useful?
    a) The one on automation was excellent. b) I have two brothers. c) Please sign here.
