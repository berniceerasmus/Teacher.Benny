Grammar: The Verb TO BE (am / is / are)
The verb "to be" is the most important verb in English.
Three forms: I AM | you / we / they ARE | he / she / it IS
I am here. / You are a student. / He or she is a teacher. / We or they are friends.
Negative: I am not tired. / She is not here. (Short forms: isn't / aren't)
Questions: Are you a student? / Is he your friend?
Short answers: Yes, I am. / No, she isn't. / Yes, they are.
Use AM only with I. Use IS with he, she, or it. Use ARE with you, we, or they. Never say 'I is' or 'she are'.

Grammar: Present Simple Tense
We use the Present Simple to talk about habits, routines, and facts. Similar to the Turkish 'geniş zaman' (-ir / -er / -ar / -ur).
I / You / We / They: use the base verb → I go, they eat.
He / She / It: add -s or -es → she goes, he watches, it works.
Negative: I don't like coffee. / She doesn't work here.
Questions: Do you eat breakfast? / Does she wake up early?
Frequency adverbs: always / usually / often / sometimes / rarely / never.
He / She / It ALWAYS gets -s! Don't forget: he drinks, she eats, it works. Also remember: Do / Does in questions.

Prepositions of place tell us WHERE something is.
The Imperative is used to give directions — use the base verb: Turn left. / Go straight.

opposite: The school is opposite the park. (karşısında)

In directions: Turn LEFT / Turn RIGHT / Go STRAIGHT / Take the FIRST turning. 'On the left / right' = a location. 'Turn left / right' = an action.

Grammar: Past Simple Tense
We use the Past Simple to talk about completed actions in the past. Similar to the Turkish '-di / -ti / -du / -tu' past tense.
Regular verbs: add -ed.   Irregular verbs: must be memorised.
Regular: visit → visited / stay → stayed / travel → travelled.
Irregular: go → went / see → saw / buy → bought / take → took.
Negative: I did not (didn't) go. / She didn't see him.
Questions: Did you enjoy it? / Where did you go?
Time words: yesterday / last week / last year / two days ago.
In Past Simple questions and negatives, the main verb returns to its base form: Did you GO? / I didn't GO. — NOT: Did you went? / I didn't went.

Grammar: Going To vs Will (Future Tense)
English has two common ways to talk about the future.
"Going to" = plans already decided before speaking: I am going to study medicine.
"Will" = a decision made at the moment of speaking, or a prediction: I think it will rain.
Negative: She won't come. / He isn't going to buy a car.
Questions: Will you help me? / What are you going to do?
Short answers: Yes, I will. / No, I won't. / Yes, she is. / No, they aren't.
'Going to' = already decided. 'Will' = decided right now, or a prediction.

Grammar: Present Perfect (have / has + past participle)
The Present Perfect connects a past event to the present moment.
Formed with: have / has + past participle (been, done, seen, taken ...)
I have had this headache since this morning. / She has seen the doctor.
Since = starting point in time: since Monday / since 9 o'clock / since last year.
For = length of time: for three days / for two hours / for a long time.
Ever / Never: Have you ever had surgery? / I have never broken a bone.
Just / Already / Yet: I have just taken my medicine. / Has she arrived yet?
Since = a specific point in time (since Monday). For = a duration (for three days). I have had this pain FOR three days / SINCE Monday.

Grammar: Modal Verbs (can / could / should / must / have to)
Modal verbs add meaning — ability, advice, obligation, or permission. They are NEVER followed by 'to' (except 'have to'). No -s for he / she / it.
Can = ability: I can speak English and Turkish.
Could = polite request / past ability: Could you send me the file, please?
Should = advice: You should arrive early for your interview.
Must = strong obligation (speaker's decision): You must bring your CV.
Have to = external obligation (a rule): I have to work overtime.
Must not = prohibition: You must not smoke in the building.
Don't have to = no obligation: You don't have to wear a suit here.
Must NOT = it is forbidden (prohibition). Don't HAVE TO = it is not necessary. These are very different meanings!

A candidate attends a professional interview.
InterviewerGood morning. Please sit down. Can you tell me about yourself?
Günaydın. Lütfen oturun. Kendinizi anlatır mısınız?

Üstesinden geldiğiniz zorlu bir duruma örnek verebilir misiniz?
CandidateI once had to meet a very tight deadline, and I managed the team well.
Bir keresinde çok sıkı bir son teslim tarihiyle karşılaştım ve takımı iyi yönettim.
InterviewerWhat are your strongest skills?
En güçlü becerileriniz neler?
CandidateI am good at communication, and I can speak Turkish and English fluently.
İletişimde iyiyim ve Türkçe ile İngilizce akıcı konuşabiliyorum.
InterviewerGreat. You should receive our decision within the week.
Kararımızı bu hafta içerisinde öğreneceksiniz.
